Proposed CRM Solution — 15 Core Features
The following section provides a detailed specification of all fifteen features proposed for Face
Contracting's CRM platform. Each feature is described in terms of its business purpose,
functional behaviour, configuration options, and data outputs.
Feature 1: Multi-Channel Lead Capture & Integration
The CRM aggregates leads from every digital touchpoint into a single, unified inbox. Instead of
sales teams checking email, social DMs, website forms, and ad platforms separately, every
inbound enquiry lands in one place — timestamped, source-tagged, and ready for assignment.
1.1 Email Integration
The system connects to corporate email inboxes via IMAP/OAuth and continuously monitors for
new messages. Emails from unknown senders are parsed and converted to leads automatically;
emails from existing contacts are threaded onto that contact'sactivity timeline.
 Two-way sync — outbound replies sent from inside the CRM are logged against the lead
 Intelligent parsing — extracts sender name, company, phone and pre-fills the lead card
 Spam and duplicate filtering — automated rules suppress known spam and internal
addresses
 Email templates — salespeople send pre-approved, personalized templates with
open/click/bounce tracking
 Attachment handling — files attached to inbound enquiries are stored against the lead
record
1.2 Social Media Integration
The CRM connects natively to Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn through their official APIs,
pulling lead data in real time from multiple sources on each platform.
Facebook
 Lead Ads — form submissions from Facebook Lead Ads are pushed to the CRM instantly
via webhook; no manual CSV download required.
 Page messages — direct messages sent to the company's Facebook Page are converted
to leads, with the conversation thread preserved and visible in the CRM.
 Comment capture — comments on specified posts (e.g. "DM us for a quote" campaigns)
that match configured keywords trigger automatic lead creation.
 Ad campaign tagging — every Facebook lead is tagged with the originating ad set and
campaign, enabling cost-per-lead reporting inside the CRM.
Instagram
=== PAGE 2 ===
 DM capture — Instagram Direct Messages to the business account are pulled in and
treated identically to Facebook messages.
 Story reply leads — replies to Instagram Stories that match lead-intent keywords are
converted to leads.
 Lead form integration — Instagram Lead Ads (run through Meta's Ads Manager) feed
into the same lead pipeline as Facebook Lead Ads.
 Profile link tracking — UTM-tagged profile link clicks route to the website integration
layer described in 1.3.
LinkedIn
 Lead Gen Forms — LinkedIn Lead Gen Form submissions (from Sponsored Content or
InMail campaigns) sync to the CRM in real time.
 InMail replies — replies to outbound InMail campaigns managed through LinkedIn Sales
Navigator are logged as lead activities.
 Company enrichment — when a LinkedIn lead is created, the CRM auto-enriches the
lead card with the prospect's job title, company size, and industry from their public
LinkedIn profile.
 Connection request tracking — connection requests sent by the salesperson to a lead
are logged as touch-points on the activity timeline
1.3 Website Integration
The CRM provides a JavaScript snippet that is embedded in the company website. Once installed,
it captures all lead-intent interactions without requiring a developer for each new form or page.
 Contact and enquiry forms — any form submission on the website (contact, get a quote,
request a demo, download a brochure) is captured as a lead with the form fields mapped
to CRM fields.
 Live chat integration — if a live chat widget is deployed, chat conversations are stored
against the lead record; if the chat ends without conversion, the partially-filled contact
details are still captured.
 Visitor tracking — for returning visitors from email links or social clicks, the CRM tracks
pages visited, time spent, and content downloaded, enriching the lead's interest profile.
1.4 Unified Lead Inbox
Regardless of source, every lead arrives in the same unified inbox with a consistent card format.
Duplicate detection runs automatically, flagging and prompting merges rather than creating
duplicate records.

Feature 5: Automatic Quotation Generation
The quotation module eliminates the manual process of building quotes in Word or Excel,
generating professional, consistently formatted quotations directly from the CRM in minutes.
Every quotation is linked to the originating lead, automatically versioned, and sent to the
prospect with a single click.
The quotation module eliminates the time-consuming manual process of building quotes from
scratch in Word or Excel, instead generating professional, consistently formatted quotations
directly from the CRM in minutes. Every quotation is linked to the originating lead, automatically
versioned, and sent to the prospect with a single click.
5.1 Quotation Creation Workflow
7. Trigger — the salesperson clicks "Generate Quote" from a lead card at any pipeline stage
from Qualified onwards.
=== PAGE 9 ===
8. Line item population — the system auto-suggests line items based on the lead's enquiry
type, the prospect's industry, and historical quotes for similar requirements (Feature 8
covers the auto-line-item logic in detail).
9. Pricing — the system suggests prices from the configured price book, adjusted by the
prospect's segment (SME / Enterprise / Government) and any pre-approved discounts for
this account type. The salesperson can override within their permitted discount limit;
overrides above their limit trigger an approval workflow (Feature 9).
10. Terms and conditions — standard T&C blocks are auto-inserted based on the
product/service type; the salesperson selects the applicable block from a library or edits
within permitted fields.
11. Review and send — the salesperson previews the rendered PDF, makes final
adjustments, and sends directly to the prospect's email from within the CRM. The quote is
simultaneously stored in the lead's document history.
12. E-signature (optional) — if configured, the quote is sent via a DocuSign/equivalent
integration, allowing the prospect to sign and return without printing.
5.2 Quotation Template Engine
 Branded templates — the company logo, colour scheme, fonts, and address block are
defined once in the template library; all generated quotes inherit them automatically.
 Multiple templates — different templates for different product lines, service types, or
customer segments (e.g. a "Formal RFQ response" template vs. a "Quick price indication"
template).
 Dynamic fields — all customer-specific details (name, company, address, reference
number, validity date) are auto-populated from the CRM lead/contact record; there are no
blanks for the salesperson to remember to fill.
 Cover letter — an auto-generated cover paragraph references the prospect's specific
enquiry, using merge fields from the lead record and a customisable template body.
 Validity and payment terms — default validity period (e.g. 30 days) and payment terms
are inserted from the configuration, overridable by the salesperson within their authority.
5.3 Quotation Numbering and Version Control
 Automatic quote number — every quotation receives a unique, sequential reference
number in the configured format (e.g. QT-2026-04821).
 Version numbering — if a quote is revised, the version suffix increments (QT-2026-
04821-v2, -v3, etc.). The original version is preserved and accessible.
 Status tracking — each quotation has a status: Draft, Sent, Viewed (if e-delivery tracking
is enabled), Accepted, Rejected, Expired.
 Expiry management — a day before a quote expires, the system auto-notifies the
salesperson to follow up or issue a revised quote.
 Lead linkage — every quotation is permanently linked to its originating lead; navigating
from the lead record to all its quotes (current and historical) is a single click.

Feature 6: Historic Quotation View
Before pricing a new quote, a salesperson needs two reference points: what was quoted to this
same client before, and what was quoted to other clients for similar scope. The historic
quotation view provides both, surfaced in context at the moment the new quote is being built.
6.1 Same-Client Historical Quotes
=== PAGE 11 ===
When building a quotation for a prospect or customer, the CRM automatically surfaces all
previous quotations issued to the same contact or company in a side panel. For each historical
quote, the salesperson sees:
 Quote reference and date — when the quote was issued and its version history.
 Services/products quoted — the line items in the historical quote, with quantities and
units.
 Pricing — the price quoted per line item, the total, and any discounts applied.
 Outcome — whether the quote was Won, Lost, Expired, or Still Open, and if Lost, the
recorded loss reason.
 Salesperson — who prepared the previous quote, enabling the current salesperson to
seek context if needed.
 Annotations — any notes attached to the quote or to the final outcome (e.g. "Client said
our price was 15% above competitor X").
💡 This view is especially valuable when a client returns after a gap — the new salesperson can see
exactly what was offered previously, at what price, and why it succeeded or failed, without
interrogating colleagues or searching email archives.
6.2 Similar-Client and Similar-Scope Quotes
Beyond the same client, the CRM maintains a searchable quote intelligence database that shows
what comparable quotes looked like across all clients. Similarity matching uses two dimensions:
 Service/product similarity — quotes that contain the same or overlapping line items or
service categories as the current quote being built.
 Client profile similarity — quotes issued to clients in the same industry vertical, company
size band, or geography as the current prospect.
The resulting "similar quotes" panel shows a range — minimum price, median price, maximum price — for
each line item type, drawn from the last 12 months of Won quotes (Won quotes only, so the benchmark
reflects prices the market actually accepted). The salesperson can click into any individual similar quote to
see the full detail if needed.
6.3 Quote Search and Filter
 Full-text search — search across all quotation content (client name, service description,
line item text) from a global search bar.
 Filter by outcome — view only Won quotes, only Lost quotes, or quotes in a specific
status.
 Filter by date range, salesperson, product category, or deal value band — slice the
quote history by any combination of these dimensions.
 Export — the filtered quote list is exportable to Excel for offline analysis.
Feature 7: Intelligent Price Recommendation
=== PAGE 12 ===
The smart pricing engine synthesises historical quote outcomes, current price book values, and
competitive signals to recommend the most appropriate price for each line item. It is a decision-
support tool — it makes a recommendation with supporting data; the salesperson makes the
final call.
7.1 Price Book Management
 Centralised price list — all standard products and services are maintained in a master price book
with list price, minimum price (floor), and standard discount limits per salesperson tier.
 Segment-based pricing — different price lists for different customer segments (SME, Enterprise,
Government, Partner) are maintained independently and applied automatically based on the lead's
segment tag.
 Time-based pricing — prices can be set to expire (e.g. promotional pricing for Q2) and revert to
standard pricing automatically on the configured end date.
 Bulk update — the entire price book can be updated via Excel upload, with a preview-and-confirm
step before changes go live.

 Payment term adjustment — the system can automatically adjust the base price based

Feature 8: Automatic Line Item Generation
The auto line item engine constructs a suggested line item list the moment a quote is initiated,
using the context of the current lead. This eliminates the risk of forgotten items, inconsistent
descriptions, and unclear scope.
8.1 How Line Items Are Suggested
Lead context — the system reads the lead's enquiry type, the services/products flagged during
qualification, and any keywords in the lead's notes or email thread.
Service/product catalogue matching — it matches these signals against the configured
product and service catalogue, selecting items whose descriptions or tags match the enquiry
context.
Bundle templates — if a matching service bundle template exists (e.g. "Annual Maintenance
Contract — HVAC" is a bundle of 4 line items), the entire bundle is suggested as a group.
Historical pattern — it checks quotes for similar clients that were Won and identifies line items
that almost always appear together (association rules), adding commonly co-included items as
suggested additions.
Salesperson review — the suggested line items appear in the quote editor as a pre-filled but
editable list. The salesperson can add, remove, reorder, and adjust quantities before finalising.

8.3 Service Bundle Templates
Bundle templates allow the organisation to pre-define common scope combinations so that
salespeople consistently quote the right scope every time, without relying on memory:
 Template library — a library of pre-built bundles, maintained by the sales admin or pre-
sales team, each containing a set of line items with default quantities and descriptions.
 Quick-select — when creating a quote, the salesperson can pick a bundle from the library

 Optional items within a bundle — items within a bundle can be marked as optional; they

 Bundle versioning — when a bundle template is updated, existing quotes using the old

Feature 10: Purchase Order (PO) Updating
=== PAGE 16 ===
When a client accepts a quote and raises a Purchase Order, the CRM transitions from a sales
tool to a delivery-handoff tool: the PO is recorded, linked to the winning quotation, and the deal
is formally closed.
10.1 PO Capture
 Manual PO entry — the salesperson records the PO number, PO date, PO value, and
PO document (PDF/scan) directly on the lead card when the client sends it.
 Email auto-detection — if the client's PO arrives as an email attachment to the
integrated inbox, the system flags the email as a potential PO and prompts the
salesperson to confirm and link it to the relevant lead.
 PO value vs. quote reconciliation — the system compares the received PO value
against the accepted quotation value and flags discrepancies (partial POs, PO with
revised scope) for the salesperson to acknowledge.
 Multi-PO support — large deals may result in multiple POs against the same agreement
(e.g. phased delivery or multi-year contracts); each PO is captured and linked individually,
and the cumulative PO value is tracked against the total deal value.
10.2 Post-PO Actions
 Lead status update — recording a PO automatically moves the lead to "Won" status if
not already there, with the PO date recorded as the close date.
 Delivery team notification — a configured notification is sent to the operations or
delivery team (by email or internal tool integration) with the PO details and linked
quotation, initiating the delivery process.
 Invoice trigger — if the CRM is integrated with the accounting system (Tally, Zoho
Books, SAP), the PO record can automatically create a proforma invoice or sales order in
the accounting system, eliminating re-entry.
 Revenue recognition — the deal value is recorded against the salesperson's quota
attainment for the month/quarter in which the PO is received.
10.3 PO Tracking and Reporting
 PO register — a filterable list of all received POs, searchable by client, salesperson, date
range, or value band. Exportable to Excel.
 Outstanding PO follow-up — when a deal is marked Won but no PO has been received
within a configured number of days, the system prompts the salesperson to follow up with
the client.
 PO-to-invoice status — if integrated with accounting, the CRM can show whether the
invoice for a given PO has been raised and paid, giving the salesperson visibility of the
full order lifecycle.
Feature 11: Activity Tracking
=== PAGE 17 ===
Activity tracking turns every interaction a salesperson has with a prospect — every call made,
email sent, meeting held, or note written — into structured, queryable data. This data serves three
purposes: it gives the salesperson a complete relationship history, it enables management to
coach based on facts rather than impressions, and it forms the input dataset for KPI reporting and
forecasting.
11.1 Activity Feed (Lead Timeline)
Every lead card has a chronological activity feed — the single, complete record of everything that
has ever happened on this lead. The feed shows:
 All logged activities in reverse-chronological order: calls, emails, meetings, notes, tasks,
stage changes, quote events (sent, viewed, approved, rejected), and PO received.
 Who did what — every entry records the CRM user responsible, enabling managers to
review any team member's activity on any lead.
 Filters — the timeline is filterable by activity type so the salesperson can quickly jump to,
for example, all emails without scrolling past every note.
 Pinned notes — important notes (e.g. "Client has a Q4 budget freeze") can be pinned to
the top of the timeline so they are visible without scrolling.
 Linked documents — quotations, PO documents, meeting attachments, and email
attachments are all accessible from the timeline without navigating to a separate
document store.
11.2 Task and Reminder System
 Task creation — tasks can be created from any lead card, assigned to self or a
colleague, with a due date and priority level.
 Recurring tasks — for standard follow-up sequences (e.g. call 2 days after sending a
quote, follow up again at 7 days), recurring task templates automate the creation of the
follow-up sequence when a quote is sent.
 Overdue task alerts — the CRM notifies the salesperson (and, after a configurable grace
period, their manager) when a task is past its due date.
 Daily task digest — each morning, the CRM sends the salesperson a summary email of
all tasks due today and all leads with no recent activity.
 Calendar sync — tasks and meetings created in the CRM sync to the salesperson's
Google Calendar or Outlook Calendar, and vice versa.

13.3 Communication Template Management
 Template library — all automated message templates are managed in a central library by
the admin; no code changes needed to update message content.
 Merge fields — templates use dynamic fields ({{lead_name}}, {{salesperson_name}},
{{quote_value}}, {{company_name}}) populated at send time from the CRM record.
 Channel selection — each template specifies which channel(s) it sends on: email, SMS,
WhatsApp Business API, or internal (push + in-app).
 Opt-out compliance — client-facing email communications include an unsubscribe link;
opt-outs are recorded in the CRM and honoured by all subsequent automations for that
contact.
 Send-time optimisation — automated emails to clients can be scheduled to send during
business hours only (e.g. not at 2 AM), configurable by the admin.
 A/B testing — for high-volume automations (e.g. lead acknowledgement), two template
variants can be A/B tested with win-condition reporting to identify the higher-performing
message.

The salesperson dashboard is the first screen a salesperson sees when they open the CRM. Its

the pipeline — without requiring the salesperson to run any reports.
